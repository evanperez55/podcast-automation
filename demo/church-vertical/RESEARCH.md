# Church Vertical Market Research — April 2026

## Market Size

- **350,000+** US churches, **320,000+** stream sermons online
- Only **1.8%** (~6,400) have searchable sermon transcripts (Pew Research)
- Church management software market: $500M-$1B, growing 12% CAGR
- Subsplash acquired by Roper Technologies for **$800M** (July 2025, 20K+ church customers)
- 52% of church leaders increasing tech budgets; tech cost concerns fell 9% YoY
- 91% of pastors support AI use in some capacity

## Pain Points (validated)

1. **Time/staff**: Pastors wear 10+ hats; no one dedicated to content repurposing
2. **No system**: Post only when someone remembers; sermons "disappear by Monday"
3. **Volunteer burnout**: Social media handled by untrained volunteers
4. **Budget constraints**: 32% lack efficient digital tools
5. **Technical skill gap**: Recording exists but editing/repurposing doesn't
6. **Content waste**: 52 sermons/year = 52 unique long-form pieces, almost none repurposed

## Ideal Customer Profile

- **Size**: 200-2,000 weekly attendance
- **Denomination**: Nondenominational, evangelical, charismatic — most tech-forward
- **Geography**: Sun Belt (TX, FL, GA, TN, NC, AZ) — fastest growth
- **Decision makers**: Communications director (feels pain) + Executive pastor (holds budget)
- **Signals**: YouTube channel with weekly full-sermon uploads, no Shorts, generic titles, low views
