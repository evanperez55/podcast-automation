# Monthly Content Release Strategy

**Release Cadence**: 1 full episode per month
**Assets per Episode**:
- 1 full episode (censored audio/video)

## Platform-Specific Strategy

### YouTube
**Priority**: High - Best for discoverability and monetization
- **Full Episode**: Release Day (Day 1)
- **Shorts**: Days 2, 9, 16 (spread across the month)
- **SEO**: Optimize titles, descriptions, tags for search
- **Thumbnails**: Consistent branding with episode number
- **Playlists**: Add to "Full Episodes" and "Best Moments" playlists

### Spotify
**Priority**: Highest - Main listening platform for podcasts
- **Full Episode**: Release Day (Day 1)
- **Consistency**: Same day/time each month builds audience habit
- **Show Notes**: Include links to YouTube, social media
- **RSS**: Auto-generated feed entries (already implemented)

### Twitter/X
**Priority**: High - Best for engagement and community building
- **Release Day**: Major announcement thread (pinned)
- **3x per week**: Clips, quotes, engagement posts
- **Daily**: Respond to mentions, retweet fan content
- **Strategy**: Mix promotional (clips/links) with conversational (questions, polls)

### TikTok
**Priority**: Medium-High - Highest viral potential
- **3 clips per month**: Days 2, 9, 16
- **Timing**: Post at peak hours (6-9 PM EST)
- **Hashtags**: Mix niche (#podcast #comedy) + trending
- **Trend Awareness**: Adapt clips to trending formats when possible
- **Bio Link**: Rotate between YouTube and Spotify

### Instagram (When Available)
**Priority**: Medium - Visual discovery
- **Reels**: Same 3 clips as TikTok (Days 2, 9, 16)
- **Stories**: Daily behind-the-scenes, clip reshares
- **Feed Posts**: Episode artwork + announcement
- **Bio Link**: Link to latest episode

## Content Distribution Rules

### The 3-Clip Strategy
Your automation creates 3 clips per episode. Here's how to maximize them:

**Clip #1** (Monday after release):
- Choose the MOST viral/shareable moment
- Platforms: YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Twitter
- Goal: Maximum reach, bring in new listeners
- Timing: First business day after Sunday launch

**Clip #2** (Second Monday - Week 2):
- Second-best moment, different topic than Clip #1
- Platforms: YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Twitter
- Goal: Re-engage audience, catch different demographics
- Timing: Keep momentum going into week 2

**Clip #3** (Third Monday - Week 3):
- Third highlight, or "fan favorite" moment
- Platforms: YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Twitter
- Goal: Final push, keep episode relevant through month
- Timing: Last clip before prep for next episode

### Cross-Promotion Matrix
Every post should guide people to your main listening platforms:

```
TikTok ‚Üí Bio link ‚Üí YouTube or Spotify
Twitter ‚Üí Direct links in tweets
YouTube Shorts ‚Üí Description link to full episode
Instagram Reels ‚Üí Bio link ‚Üí Latest episode
```

### Posting Times (EST - Adjust for your timezone)
- **YouTube**: 12 PM (lunch break viewing)
- **Twitter**: 9 AM, 3 PM, 8 PM (commute + evening)
- **TikTok**: 6-9 PM (peak engagement hours)
- **Instagram**: 11 AM, 7 PM

## Tips for Monthly Release Success

### 1. Consistency is Key
Release on the **last Sunday of every month**. This builds audience anticipation and habit.
People will know: "New Fake Problems episode every last Sunday."

### 2. Quality Over Quantity
With monthly releases, each episode needs to be strong. The automated Claude analysis helps ensure you're highlighting the best moments.

### 3. Evergreen Content
Since episodes are monthly, make content that stays relevant beyond release week. Avoid time-sensitive references unless necessary.

### 4. Build a Backlog
Once you have 3-4 months of episodes, you can reshare old clips during "off weeks" to maintain presence.

### 5. Engage, Don't Just Broadcast
Respond to every comment you can. Monthly releases mean you have time to build community between episodes.

### 6. Experiment with Timing
Track which posting times get best engagement for YOUR specific audience. Adjust the schedule based on your analytics.

### 7. Cross-Platform Promotion
Don't assume your Twitter audience follows you on TikTok. Mention other platforms occasionally to build omnichannel presence.

## Adapting This Strategy

As your podcast grows, you can:
- Increase posting frequency (e.g., 2x per week instead of weekly clips)
- Add more clip variations (different lengths, formats)
- Introduce bonus content (blooper reels, extended cuts)
- Go live on occasion (Twitter Spaces, YouTube live)
- Create clip compilations ("Best of 2025" videos)

This strategy ensures consistent presence despite monthly episodes while making the most of your automation's output.
