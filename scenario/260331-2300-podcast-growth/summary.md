# Growth Strategy Summary — Fake Problems Podcast

## The Diagnosis

You built a Ferrari production pipeline but you're parked in a garage. The automation is world-class — the problem is that **nobody knows you exist**. This is normal for 95% of podcasts. The good news: you have the infrastructure to execute on any of these strategies immediately.

## The 5 Things To Do This Week

### 1. Fix your clips (2 hours)
- Extend `CLIP_MAX_DURATION` to 60 seconds
- Add text overlay hooks to the first 2 seconds of every clip
- Post 3-5 clips per episode instead of the "best" ones
- Add trending hashtags to every Short/Reel/TikTok

### 2. Fix your YouTube titles (30 minutes)
- Format: `[Searchable topic] | Fake Problems Podcast`
- Not: "Lobsters Are Basically Immortal" (funny but unsearchable)
- Yes: "The Animal That Can't Die | Fake Problems Podcast"

### 3. Create a pinned "start here" clip (1 hour)
- 60-second highlight reel of your funniest moments
- Pin it on TikTok, Instagram, Twitter, YouTube channel trailer
- This is what new visitors see first

### 4. Pitch yourself as a guest on 3 podcasts (1 hour)
- Find 3 comedy podcasts at your level (500-5000 listeners)
- Use your prospect_finder.py — it's literally built for this
- Send a short, genuine pitch

### 5. Start "Fake Problem of the Day" posts (ongoing, 5 min/day)
- One funny observation per day on Twitter + Instagram
- No podcast link needed — just build the brand
- "Fake Problem: My ice cream melted before I finished my existential crisis"

## The 5 Things To Do This Month

1. **Guest on 2+ other podcasts** (proven #1 growth strategy)
2. **Claim profiles** on Spotify for Podcasters, Apple Podcasts Connect, YouTube Podcasts
3. **Reddit AMA** in r/podcasts about your AI production pipeline
4. **Create a "Best Of" compilation** video from 30 episodes
5. **Start an email list** (Substack — free, takes 10 minutes to set up)

## What NOT To Do

- Don't buy followers or listens — they destroy your algorithmic signals
- Don't post the same clip on every platform without adaptation — each platform rewards different formats
- Don't spam subreddits or Discord servers with links — build genuine presence first
- Don't focus on production quality — yours is already great. Focus on distribution and engagement.
- Don't wait until your audience is "big enough" to collaborate — start now at any size

## Your Unfair Advantage

You have something 99% of podcasts don't: **automated production infrastructure**. Use it:
- You can post 5 clips per episode where others post 1
- You can A/B test titles by posting the same clip with different hooks
- You can generate blog posts, show notes, and social captions at zero marginal cost
- You can react to trending topics with clips within hours, not days

The pipeline isn't the product — it's the **distribution weapon**. Point it at the right targets.
